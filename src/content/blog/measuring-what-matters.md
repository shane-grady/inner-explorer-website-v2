Schools are accountable for outcomes, and mindfulness should be no exception. The
question isn't whether students enjoy the practice — it's whether it helps.

## What the evidence shows

Studies of school-based mindfulness link regular practice to improvements in
attention, emotional regulation, and classroom climate. The strongest results come
from programs that are implemented consistently and with fidelity.

## Tracking it in your school

Pair daily practice with simple, repeatable measures — attendance, office referrals,
and brief student check-ins — to see the trend over a semester rather than a day.
